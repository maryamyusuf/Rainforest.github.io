      Homeless Animals?

      Are we taking the home from animals?
      Yes
      The Rainforest is home to many different exotic animals some of which are endangered and losing their homes due to deforestation.
      Some animals that are going extinct are:

        • Jaguars
        • Giant Otters
        • Various Breeds of Monkeys
        • Poisinous Frogs
        • Many different birds

        These animals are essential to keep food-chains stable as well as help out the habitat they stay in.
        The monkeys also help the Rainforest in a speacial way called Zoophily.
        The Rainforest has around 40,000 plant species, nearly 1,300 bird species, 3,000 types of fish, 427 species of mammals, and 2.5 million different insects.

        Learning more about the animals in the Rainforest can help us better our understanding and educate others!

        We can make a difference? How?

        But isn't it too late?
        Not at all!
        Our Rainforests have been through a lot and are not in the best state, but we can still help!
        Some minor things you can do to help are:

          • You can save rainforests by reducing your use of products that are grown on cleared rainforest lands.
          • Go paperless at home and in the office.
          • Buy recycled products and then recycle them again.
          • Buy certified wood products. Read the labels and look for the FSC mark.
          • Raise awareness in your circle and in your community.

          And if you aren't able to do any of these...
          You can donate to different foundations that help preserve and save the Rainforest.
          One popular foundation to donate to is Rainforest Trust
          If you want to try and Make a Difference, start today and be a part of the change!
